I've spent years running first-principles calculations on 3d transition metal chalcogenides — accurate, but slow. A converged DFT+U run with proper magnetic configuration testing can take days. Over the years I've accumulated dozens of compounds' worth of results, and the trends across them are often more interesting than any single result.

Why does one MTe compound open a gap while a structurally similar MSe doesn't? Why does magnetic ordering flip from antiferromagnetic to ferromagnetic when the M–X–M angle crosses some threshold? I know the qualitative physics — Goodenough-Kanamori-Anderson rules, crystal field theory, Zaanen-Sawatzky-Allen classification — but I've never had a systematic, quantitative cross-compound view.

Physics-informed, not black-box

A model that outputs "band gap = 0.8 eV" with no explanation is useless to me. So the approach: encode the descriptors I already trust — bond angles, crystal field splitting, d-electron count, electronegativity differences — as explicit features, then fit interpretable models I can check against the physics.

What this series will document

Build a unified dataset (own DFT + literature + Materials Project) → engineer physics-informed descriptors → look for structure → electronic relationships → then structure → magnetic → then the coupling between the two → symbolic regression for formula discovery → a rule bank for predicting unknown compounds.

If it works, the payoff is concrete: faster screening of candidate MC compounds, better-targeted DFT calculations, and a few rules worth publishing alongside the usual first-principles results.
